Warm Up: Font Feelings Speed Match

Poetry Font Speed Dating!

In pairs, you have 3 minutes to explore how fonts create feelings:

  1. Open a new Google Doc on your iPad
  2. Type one emotion word (happy, sad, scary, calm)
  3. Highlight it and tap the font menu - try 3 different fonts
  4. Quick share with your partner: "I chose [font] because..."

🤔 Key Question

How can the WAY words look change the way they FEEL?

Teacher tip: Circulate quickly and highlight good font choices. Use font emotion examples on the board if students need inspiration.

Launch: The Digital Poet's Toolkit (I Do)

Watch me transform a poem from ordinary to extraordinary on my iPad!

I'll demonstrate with our class colour poem about "Grey":

📝 Step 1: Type with Purpose

  • Watch how I tap 'return' once for line breaks within stanzas
  • Double tap 'return' creates space between stanzas
  • Title goes at top, name at bottom

🎨 Step 2: Font as Mood-Maker

"My grey poem feels quiet and a bit lonely, like fog... Let me highlight the text and explore fonts. Comic Sans is too playful! This 'Merriweather' font feels more serious and matches my mood better."

🎨 Step 3: Colour with Care

"I'll highlight my text, tap the 'A' with the coloured line underneath. Black feels too harsh - let me try this dark grey. See how it softens the feeling?"

📸 Step 4: The Perfect Picture

"Tap Insert → Image → Search the web. I could search 'grey paint' but that's boring! My poem mentions 'morning mist' so I'll search for 'foggy path' - this image captures the lonely feeling perfectly."

🎯 Remember

Every choice should support your poem's message!

Launch: Digital Design Rules (We Do)

Let's create our Digital Poetry Golden Rules together!

Look at these two Google Docs - which is easier to read and why?

Our Class Design Charter

  • Maximum 2 fonts (title + body)
  • Maximum 3 colours (including black)
  • White space is your friend - poems need room to breathe!
  • One meaningful image only - it supports, not steals the show

✨ Design Principle

Less is more - clean design lets your poetry shine!

Show the before/after examples, get student agreement on rules...

Explore: Create Your Digital Masterpiece (You Do)

Your Mission: Bring one poem to digital life!

  1. Open Google Docs on your iPad
  2. Choose your favourite poem from your anthology (acrostic, cinquain, or colour poem)
  3. Type it carefully - think about where lines break for meaning
  4. Format with purpose: Highlight text → tap font menu → choose one that matches your poem's personality
  5. Tap the 'A' button → pick colours that enhance (not distract)
  6. Tap Insert → Image → Search the web → add ONE image that deepens meaning
  7. Your doc auto-saves, but rename it: Tap the title → "PoemTitle_YourName"

🤝 Support Station - Poetry Apprentice

  • Open the shared "Poetry Template" in our class Google Drive folder
  • Follow the checklist on your table
  • Focus on just font and spacing first - add image if time allows
  • Tap the hand icon if you need tech help

🚀 Challenge Corner - Poetry Design Pro

  • Try Format → Paragraph styles for fancy title effects
  • Explore Insert → Drawing to create word art
  • Add a text box with a poet's note about your inspiration
  • Use Insert → Table (1x1) to create a decorative border

iPad Quick Tips

Font Guide: Serif fonts (like Times) feel formal, Sans-serif (like Arial) feel modern
Touch Gestures: Two-finger tap = undo, Pinch to zoom for detailed work
Image Search: Use descriptive keywords that match your poem's mood

iPad/Google Docs Shortcuts

Line Breaks: Single return = line break (same stanza), Double return = new stanza
Undo: Two-finger tap = undo, Pinch to zoom for detailed work
Formatting: Select text → Format menu appears automatically

🛠️ Troubleshooting Tips

  • Font menu not showing: Check text is highlighted
  • Image won't move: Tap image → three dots → "Wrap text"
  • Can't find document: It's auto-saved in Drive under "Recent"
  • Insert menu missing: Make sure they're in edit mode (pencil icon)
