Session 8: Colour Poems

A Sensory Adventure

Learning Intent & Success Criteria

Learning Intention:

I am learning to create vivid colour poems that bring colours to life using all my senses and original word choices.

Success Criteria:

  • I can describe a colour using at least three different senses (not just sight)
  • I can dodge overused comparisons and create fresh, surprising images
  • I can choose words that build a specific mood or feeling
⏱️ 5:00

Warm Up: Sensory Colour Detective Sprint

The Challenge:

In pairs, you'll play "Colour Detective"!

  1. Detective closes their eyes
  2. Partner picks a colour card (don't tell!)
  3. Partner gives rapid-fire sensory clues - 30 seconds:
    • "It sounds like..."
    • "It tastes like..."
    • "It feels like..."
    • "It smells like..."
  4. Detective guesses the colour!
  5. Switch roles - GO!
Teacher tip: Use basic colour cards (red, blue, yellow, green, purple, orange). Model one quick example first!
⏱️ 8:00

Launch: Breaking Out of Cliché Jail

The Great Word Heist - "I Do"

Watch as I bust Purple out of Cliché Jail!

❌ First, let's lock up the boring suspects:

  • Grapes 🚫
  • Royalty 🚫
  • Flowers 🚫

✨ Create Fresh Images

🎨 Build Mood

Now, my jailbreak poem:

Purple
👅Tastes like frozen blackberries before they're sweet
👂Sounds like static on an old TV at midnight
✋Feels like velvet rubbed backwards - tiny electric sparks
👃Smells like thunderstorms trapped in glass jars
💭Is the breath you hold before blowing out birthday candles
Notice how this taste is specific and connects to anticipation - not just "sweet like grapes"
This sound creates a mysterious, late-night mood - unexpected for a colour!
The texture creates a physical sensation that matches purple's luxurious feel
This smell evokes power and mystery - perfect for purple's mood
The emotion ties everything together - purple as anticipation and magic

Notice how I:

  • 🌟Used ALL senses + emotion
  • 📖Created mini-stories in each line
  • 🎭Built a mysterious, electric mood
  • ⚡Kept every image unexpected
⏱️ 7:00

Launch: Collaborative Word Heist

The Great Red Robbery - "We Do"

Let's steal some amazing words for RED together!

Step 1: Lock up the clichés! (30 seconds)

What boring red images should go to jail? (roses, blood, stop signs, hearts)

Step 2: The Sensory Heist (4 minutes)

  • Sounds like: siren's wail, popcorn popping, angry whisper
  • Tastes like: chilli's sting, rust on a spoon, strawberry seeds
  • Feels like: sunburn's tight skin, embarrassment creeping up your neck
  • Smells like: hot car metal, mum's lipstick, autumn bonfire

Step 3: Quick Practice (2 minutes)

Turn to your partner - create ONE line about red using our stolen words!

⏱️ 15:00

Explore: Your Colour Creation

Your Sensory Mission - "You Do"

Your Task:

  1. Choose your colour (card or your own choice)
  2. Brainstorm sensory connections (2 mins)
  3. Draft your 5-7 line poem (10 mins)
  4. Highlight your strongest sensory line (1 min) - use highlighters
  5. Share with your partner - get one "glow" feedback (2 mins)

Remember: Each line = one sense or feeling. No clichés allowed!

Review: Colour Gallery & Glow Notes

Gallery Celebration

Your Gallery Mission:

  1. Display your poem on your desk
  2. Take 2 sticky notes
  3. Silent gallery walk - read at least 3 poems
  4. Leave "Glow Notes" on your 2 favourites:
    • Star the strongest sensory line
    • Name the mood it created
⭐ Example Glow Note

Closing Circle (2 mins)

Stand if your poem includes:

  • A taste that surprised you
  • A sound no one else would think of
  • An emotion hidden in a colour
  • A line that makes YOU proud

Exit Question:

Which sense was hardest to write? Which was most fun?
