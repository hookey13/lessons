Learning Intent & Success Criteria

Learning Intent:

I am learning to create acrostic poems that paint vivid pictures using powerful vocabulary choices.

Success Criteria:

  • I can craft an acrostic poem where each line connects to create one unified story or feeling
  • I can upgrade ordinary words to extraordinary vocabulary that makes readers feel something
  • I can use editing strategies to polish my poem until it shines
Teacher Guidance for Learning Intent:

Have students click each success criteria checkbox as they understand it. Emphasize that this isn't about listing words - it's about creating a unified picture with powerful vocabulary.

Warm Up: Vocabulary Lightning Exchange

⏱️ 5:00

Alright poets, let's wake up those word brains!

Here's how Vocabulary Lightning Exchange works:

  1. You've got 30 seconds with each partner
  2. Partner A says a basic word (like big or happy)
  3. Partner B fires back the most creative synonym they can think of (colossal or euphoric)
  4. When you hear the bell - DING! - rotate to your next partner and switch roles

Final Challenge Round:

As a class, let's upgrade these tired words:

  • nice → ?
  • said → ?
  • went → ?

Trust me, this is going to power up your poems in ways you can't imagine yet!

Teacher Guidance for Warm Up:

Pre-plan mixed ability pairs. Have a bell or timer ready for 30-second rotations. Keep energy high - this sets the tone for creative vocabulary use throughout the lesson.

Launch: The Acrostic Transformation

⏱️ 8:00

Okay, watch this magic happen. Here's what most people think an acrostic poem is:

❌ Boring List Poem:

Sky is dark
Thunder is loud
Outside is wet
Rain falls down
My umbrella is up

Boring, right? It's just a list dressed up as a poem. Now watch what happens when we actually craft it like poets:

✨ Crafted Acrostic Poem:

Sinister clouds devour the daylight
Thunderclaps shatter the silence
Ominous winds whisper urgent warnings
Relentless raindrops percussion the pavement
Monumental power unleashed from above

Can you feel the difference? The second one puts you RIGHT in that storm. Every line works together to create one powerful experience. That's what we're aiming for today - not just words that start with letters, but lines that create magic together.

Quick Check: Turn to your partner - which specific words made the biggest difference? Why?
Teacher Guidance for Launch (I Do):

Read both poems aloud with different energy levels. For the boring one, use a flat monotone. For the crafted one, use dramatic expression. The contrast helps students feel the difference powerful vocabulary makes.

Explore: Your Poetry Workshop

⏱️ 20:00

Your Mission: Create TWO Acrostic Masterpieces

Poem 1: Your Name (8 minutes)

This is your poetic fingerprint - a poem that captures who you are, what you dream about, or what makes you unique. No one else can write this poem because no one else is you!

Poem 2: Your Choice (7 minutes)

Pick something you're passionate about:

  • A place that matters to you BEACH, SPACE, HOME
  • A feeling you know well BRAVE, PEACE, HAPPY
  • Something you love GAMING, MUSIC, SPORT, BOOKS

The Editing Power-Up (5 minutes)

Find your editing partner and use the VIBE check:

  • Vocabulary: Circle one word to upgrade together
  • Idea: Does every line connect to the theme?
  • Beginning letters: All correct and natural?
  • Ending: Does the last line leave an impact?

✓ Enabler Support:

Start with a 4-letter word or nickname. Focus on making each line at least 3 words long. Use the vocabulary starter cards on your table for inspiration.

Need help getting started? Get Support Strips

⚡ Extender Challenge:

Create a reverse acrostic (last letters spell your word) OR make each line exactly 7 syllables for a haiku-acrostic fusion!

Differentiation Quick Reference:

For students needing support: Direct to enabler strips, pair with confident peer, accept shorter poems (3-4 letters), focus on one strong vocabulary upgrade per poem.

For extension students: Challenge with reverse acrostics, syllable constraints, or theme restrictions. Have them mentor others during editing phase.

Teacher Guidance for Explore:

Circulate actively during independent work. Look for students using basic vocabulary and prompt with "What's a more powerful word for...?". During VIBE check partnerships, ensure students are giving specific feedback, not just "It's good".

Quick whip-around - share one of these:

  • Your proudest vocabulary upgrade
  • A new word you discovered today
  • Which classmate's theme surprised you most

Exit Ticket (1 minute):

On your way out, drop a note in the box answering: "How did upgrading my vocabulary change the power of my poem?"

Don't forget: Choose your best acrostic to polish for your poetry anthology. These are going to look amazing in your published collection!

Quick Assessment Indicators:
  • Achieving: Creates unified poems where all lines connect to theme, uses 3+ vocabulary upgrades, helps peers meaningfully
  • Developing: Creates basic acrostics with 1-2 vocabulary upgrades, may need prompting to connect lines thematically
  • Needs Support: Struggles with structure, relies on basic vocabulary, needs scaffolding for line creation

Exit Ticket Analysis: Look for students who can articulate HOW vocabulary changes impact - this shows metacognitive understanding.

Teacher Guidance for Review:

During gallery walk, model specific praise: "I love how Sarah used 'devoured' instead of 'ate' - it creates such a vivid image!". Keep exit ticket responses for assessment data on vocabulary growth.
